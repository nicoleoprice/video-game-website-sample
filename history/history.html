The History of Gaming

1940's

Nimatron

    One of the very first video games that existed was Nimatron, or Nim for short. Nim was invented by Edward U. Condon. The premise of the game is to turn off the lightbulbs. Whoever turns off the last light wins. It is a single player game against the computer where the player turns off ine or more lights in a single round then the computer takes a turn. It was widely popular st the time.

Cathode Ray Tube

    In the late 1940's, another game by the name of “Cathode Ray Tube Amusement Device” was patented by Thomas T. Goldsmith Jr. and Estle Ray Mann. This device is considered one of the first interactive computer games. In this game, the player points a beam from the cathode ray tube at a screen that has an overlay of different targets. The object of the game is to shoot the targets by pressing a button.

1960's

Sports

    Computer games based off of sports grew in popularity. Some of the games were based on baseball or football.

Spacewar!

    Spacewar! was the very first computer-based game. Prior to Spacewar! being invented in 1962, electronic games were based on games that already existed in the physical world, such as sports and board games. This game changed the way video games were made and it encouraged more imaginative games.

Magnavox Odyssey

    The Magnavox Odyssey was the very first home video game system. It took five years to develop and was based on Ralph H. Baer's designs. The idea was conceived in 1966 and the final product was released in 1971.

1970's

Oregon Trail

    The Oregon Trail was developed by Don Rawitsch, Bill Heinemann, and Paul Dillenberger in 1971. They sent it to the Minnesota Educational Computer Cosortium (MECC) and from there it was nationally distributed. It became one of the most widely successful educational games.

Galaxy Game

    The earliest coin-operated video arcade game was Galaxy Game. It was installed in 1971. This was the start of the arcade era of gaming.

1980's

Donkey Kong

    Nintendo releases Donkey Kong in 1981. This is the game where Mario is first introduced, which catapaulted Nintendo's popularity and Mario becomes the star of the company.

Game Boy

    Nintendo's Game Boy was released in 1989 and it popularized handheld gaming. It was not the first portable video game, which was invented a decade before, but this popularity inspired several newer portable gaming systems in years ahead.

1990's

Solitaire

    When Microsoft released Windows 3.0, they bundled some video games, such as Bejeweled, with it. Solitaire was one of those games. It is a simplistic card game designed for those who would not normally gravitate towards console gaming. Solitaire became one of the most popular computer games ever.

Violence in Video Games

    In 1993, videogames, such as Mortal Kombat, prompts the United States Senate hearings regarding the concerns of violent videogames. This was also the same year where Doom popularized first-person shooter games.

PlayStation

    The first PlayStation was released by the Japanese-based company Sony in the United States in 1995. It was $100 cheaper than the Sega Saturn, which in today's money, it would be $174. Sega Saturn was developed by the American-based company Sega. With the release of PlayStation and Nintendo 64 (N64) in the following year, Sega's home console business was weakened.

2000's

The Sims

    Although The Sims is not the first simulation game created, it became one of the best-selling computer games and it was most popular among female players.

Xbox

    In 2001, Microsoft entered the game industry and created the console system Xbox.

Steam

    In 2003, Valve releases a digital platform for PC gaming called Steam. Players can download, play, and update games. To learn more about PC gaming, click here.

Nintendo DS

    In 2004, Nintendo dominates the market with this portable, handheld device with two screens and two processors.

Nintendo Wii

    This console, that was released in 2006, encouraged players to be physically active while playing the games with the motion-sensitive wireless remotes.

World of Warcraft

    World of Warcraft was released in 2004, but in 2008 it became the most popular Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) ever.

2010's

Minecraft

    The rise of indie game developers boosts the popularity of indie games. Minecraft, developed by Swedish-developer Markus Persson, is one of those indie games.

Mature Video Game Storylines

    In 2013, several games like The Last of Us and Gone Home influenced a new type of videogames featuring complex storylines where the player has to make difficult emotional decisions on what happens in the game.

Pokemon Go

    The augmented reality game Pokemon Go encouraged players to go outside in the summer of 2016. This game incorporated real-life scenery with animated creatures.

Nintendo Switch

    The Nintendo Switch hits the market and is the first system that is a hybrid of a portable device, in the form of a tablet, and a home console system.

Fortnite

    Epic Games' online battle royale game Fortnite gains popularity and becomes one of the most popular games in 2019.

2020's

Multiplayer Games

    With the coronavirus pandemic keeping people at home in 2020, many multiplayer games became popular and allowed players to connect while in quarantine. Some of these games are Animal Crossing: New Horizons, Among Us, and Fall Guys.
