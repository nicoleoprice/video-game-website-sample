Producer

Types of Producers

    A general role of a producer is to deal with management, planning, commissioning, and funding game projects. There are two different types of producers in the gaming industry.

External Producer
This type of producer works with the publishing company rather than the developers. They are involved in commissioning games towards release. Instead of being involved in day-to-day business activities, they approve of milestones, managing payment schedules to developers, and organizing quality assurance within the publishing company.
Internal Producer
This type of producer works within the game studio as managers of the team. They are the boss of project management, costs, and issues with human resources. Some studios have a separation between the producer and game director roles (where the producer does not have a creative role), while other studios have the producer as a creative lead.
